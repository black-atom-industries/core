Frutiger Aero

An entirely blue-green harmonic · No warm intruders

🫧 Collection: Aero 🌿 dark 💧 cool-only palette

Concept

A theme that captures the earnest, utopian techno-optimism of Frutiger Aero (2004–2013) — the era of Windows Vista's glass effects, iPod commercials, and the belief that technology would harmonize with nature. It's the after of Y2K: instead of chrome raves and plastic toys, we get deep blue water, translucent glass interfaces, and a calm, humanist digital atmosphere.

The palette is entirely cool — blue, green, and purple harmonics only. No reds, no yellows, no warm oranges. Every accent color lives within the blue-green spectrum, descending from ocean abyss up through teal, aqua, sky, and seafoam toward frost white.

Core feeling: Submerged in a bioluminescent ocean at twilight. The UI is made of frosted glass panels floating in deep water. Light filters down from above in shades of sky blue and seafoam green. Nothing disrupts the cool temperature — not strings, not types, not even errors. The only hint of warmth is a desaturated rose for errors, barely above the cool threshold.

Aesthetic Principles

🫧 Glass, Not Plastic

Where Y2K was translucent plastic, Aero is frosted glass. Soft inner glow, beveled reflections, layered transparency. UI elements float above the abyss.

🌊 Entirely Cool Temperature

No warm colors anywhere. Background to text, keywords to strings — everything stays in the blue-green-purple spectrum. The only visual "warmth" is the relative brightness of sky and seafoam against dark teal.

🌿 Nature + Technology Harmony

Frutiger Aero imagined technology blending with the natural world. Leaf and Seafoam aren't just colors — they reference the bokeh grass, water, and sky imagery that defined the aesthetic.

✨ Glossy Optimism

Not the nervous energy of Y2K or the sterile precision of modern minimalism. Every surface has a gentle sheen — the promise that the future is clean and beautiful.

🔷 Seafoam Replaces Coral

Strings shift from pink (#FF7B7B) to mint (#66D9C0). Seafoam is the cool answer to "data" — distinct from Sky (control flow) and Leaf (action), but never leaving the blue-green family.

💜 Periwinkle Replaces Sunset

Types shift from orange (#F7B83B) to blue-purple (#8AB4E8). Periwinkle sits between Lavender (numbers) and Sky (keywords) — distinct without breaking temperature.

Light Appearance Notes

Light variant inverts the depth: background becomes a pale sky-adjacent cream (#E8F0E8), text goes to deep ocean (#082838). Aqua and Sky retain their roles but sit on pale backgrounds — like looking up through the water surface toward a clear sky.

The accent colors shift +15% brightness for contrast against light backgrounds. Seafoam becomes #88E8D0, Periwinkle becomes #A8CCF0. The cool harmony stays intact — just brighter, airier.

Rough light direction: BG ~#E8F0E8, text ~#082838, backgrounds shift toward "beach glass on pale sand." The feel is "sunlight through clear ocean water."

Design Inspirations

  • Windows Vista / Windows 7 Aero Glass — the translucent title bars, the start button glow, the aqua orb
  • iPod touch / iPhone OS 1–3 — glossy icons, rounded glass, the "slide to unlock" sheen
  • Mac OS X Tiger → Snow Leopard — Aqua buttons, translucent menus, water droplet aesthetic
  • Wii Menu / Xbox 360 "Blades" — channel-based UI, glossy panels, friendly roundedness
  • Nintendo DS / DSi — dual-screen optimism, stylus-friendly rounded UI
  • Frutiger typeface — clean, humanist, the font of airport signage and optimistic public spaces
  • BioShock (2007) — art deco underwater utopia, the blue-green Rapture lighting
  • Jetsetradio / Amped 3 — the glossy, colorful high-contrast cool of mid-2000s games
