Y2K iMac

Bondi Blue · Translucent Plastics · Candy-Colored Optimism

🖥️ Collection: Retro

Concept

Designing a theme that captures the earnest, techno-optimistic spirit of the iMac G3 era (1998–2001). Where Y2K Chrome is nightclubs and raves, Y2K iMac is the glow of a Bondi Blue CRT in a teenager's bedroom — the promise of the internet, AOL CDs, and "Think Different."

Core feeling: Translucent, cheerful, tactile. Colors feel like candy shells and soft plastics. The UI should look like iMac branding — rounded, glossy, inviting — without being cutesy or childish.

Aesthetic Principles

1. Translucency Over Opacity

Where conventional themes use solid backgrounds, Y2K iMac should feel slightly see-through. Think the translucent teal plastic of an original iMac — you can almost see the cathode ray tube inside. In practice: use subtle alpha overlays, glow effects on borders, and gradient backgrounds that suggest depth behind the surface.

2. Warm CRT Glow

Not cold cyberpunk blue. The background is a warm charcoal (#0A1515 has a hint of teal warmth), not the clinical black of modern dark themes. Syntax highlighting should feel like glowing phosphor, not LED backlight.

3. Candy-Colored Restraint

The five iMac colors are the entire accent palette. No infinite color wheel — you work with what's in the candy box. This forces intentionality: every color choice is a direct reference to a physical iMac G3 color. That's the constraint that makes it cohesive.

4. Plastics, not Metals

Y2K Chrome is silver and foil. Y2K iMac is colored plastic — matte shells with glossy highlights, not metallic reflections. Avoid chrome gradients, brushed metal textures, and cold grays.

5. Optimism, not Nostalgia

The iMac wasn't nostalgic when it launched — it was forward-looking. The theme should feel hopeful, not sentimental. Bright accent colors on a dark but warm background: the feeling of turning on a computer in 1999 and seeing the future.

Light Appearance Notes

If a light variant is desired, the principle inverts: keep the Bondi Blue and candy accents, but the background becomes a warm off-white (think iMac's platinum white bezel) with the candy colors gaining slightly more chroma to hold their presence against white.

Rough light palette direction: BG ~#F5F0E8 (warm platinum), text ~#1A3A3A (deep teal-black), candy accents as-is but shifted 5% brighter. The feel should be "a white iMac G3 on a sunlit desk."

Design Inspirations

  • 🍏 iMac G3 (1998) — the Bondi Blue original, Jony Ive's translucent revolution
  • 💻 Mac OS 8/9 Platinum — the chiseled, rounded interface that preceded Aqua
  • 🫧 Original iBook "Clamshell" — Tangerine and Blueberry translucent plastics
  • 🎬 Apple "Think Different" campaign — the optimism of late-90s industrial design
  • 📺 Toonami / Cartoon Network — the broadcast design language of the millennium era
  • 🎮 Nintendo 64 / PS1 UI — chunky, colorful, low-poly, earnest
